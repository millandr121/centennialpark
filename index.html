Getting here

The journey is
half the trip.

Bamfield sits at the end of the road. Drive in, sail the inlet, or fly low over the sound — pick your adventure below and watch the route draw itself.

Port Alberni → Bamfield 90 km · about 1 hr 40 min

★ The route we recommend for everyone. Fully chipsealed and well maintained all the way in. If you're towing, bringing an RV or camper, or it's your first trip — take this one.
  • Fill up in Port Alberni — no gas on the road.
  • Yellow km markers count you down the whole way.
  • Logging trucks have right of way; lights on, slow for one-lane bridges.

Duncan → Bamfield via Youbou · about 2 hr 30 min

⚠ Active logging road past Youbou. Speeds can drop to 10–20 km/h on rough stretches and grading is unpredictable. Not recommended for RVs, campers, trailers, or first-time visitors — take Port Alberni instead.
  • Hwy 18 is paved to the Youbou area; chipseal returns ~km 32 into Bamfield.
  • Fuel up in Duncan or Lake Cowichan — Youbou's gas bar keeps limited hours.

Port Renfrew / Nitinat 185 km · about 3 hr 45 min

⚠ Active logging road from Nitinat. Expect 10–20 km/h on ungraded sections; maintenance isn't scheduled. RVs, campers, and first-timers should take the Port Alberni route.
  • Best only if you're already on the south coast (Renfrew, Sooke, Victoria west).
  • Allow generous extra time — conditions vary day to day.

The M/V Francis Barkley

A working packet freighter that's been threading the Alberni Inlet for decades — half ferry, half postal run, all west coast. Sails Tue, Thu, Sat (plus summer extras). Get off at the East dock for the park.
