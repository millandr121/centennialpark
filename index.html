Getting here

The journey is
half the trip.

Bamfield sits at the end of the road. Drive in, sail the inlet, or fly low over the sound — pick your adventure below and watch the route draw itself.

Port Alberni → Bamfield 90 km · about 1 hr 40 min

It is highly recommended to take this route regardless of your starting point — whether you're coming from Victoria, Duncan, Sooke, or anywhere else on the island. Fully chip-sealed and well maintained all the way in.
  • Fill up in Port Alberni — it is truly the last gas stop before Bamfield.
  • Yellow km markers count you down the whole way.
  • Logging trucks have right of way — keep lights on and slow for one-lane bridges.

Lake Cowichan / Youbou Logging Road via south shore · about 2 hr 30 min

Highly not recommended unless you are very familiar with it. Highway 18 is paved from Lake Cowichan to the Youbou area. From Youbou onward it is an active industrial logging road — very rough, speeds as low as 10–20 km/h, no maintenance schedule. Not suitable for RVs, campers, trailers, or any first-time visitors.
  • Start from Lake Cowichan on Highway 18 — not from South side of Lake Cowichan.
  • Fuel up in Lake Cowichan, or at Youbou's Daly's Auto Centre (limited hours) — the last fuel before the logging road.
  • The road is chip-sealed again around km 34 (the markers count up from Port Alberni, not Bamfield), but the middle section is rough logging road.

Port Renfrew — Safer Route via Lake Cowichan + Port Alberni · about 3 hr 15 min

If you are starting from the Jordan River / Port Renfrew area, take this route. There are several ways to reach Bamfield from Port Renfrew, but this is the only one that is all chip-seal or pavement — every other option runs on active logging roads. Head north to Lake Cowichan — the road from Port Renfrew to Lake Cowichan is chip-sealed, a smaller winding forest road. From Lake Cowichan continue to North Cowichan and Highway 1, then up to Port Alberni and take the chip-seal road in. This saves time versus doubling back to Victoria.
  • Port Renfrew → Lake Cowichan: chip-sealed, winding forest road — safe but takes about 50 min.
  • Lake Cowichan → Hwy 1 → Port Alberni → Bamfield: all chip-sealed or paved highway from here.
  • Do not turn off toward Youbou — stay on the highway north to Port Alberni.

Good to know

Park info & rules

Check-in & check-out

  • Check-in 2:00 PM · Check-out 11:00 AM. Please be off your site by 11 AM on departure day.
  • Late-night drive-ins without a reservation: find a vacant site and register at the office once we're open. You may be asked to move if the site is booked for the next day.

On your site

  • One tent/camper per site. Additional tents or campers, where allowed, are an extra charge.
  • No tents in the playground area.
  • Keep tables & fire pits in your site — please don't move them.
  • Please respect the privacy of other campers.

Quiet time

  • Quiet hours from 10:00 PM. Excessive noise or partying earns a warning and a noise-disturbance fee; continued disturbance can mean eviction with no refund.

Fires & firewood

  • Campfires are allowed when there is no fire ban in effect.
  • Do not cut wood in the forest.

Garbage

  • Pack out what you pack in. The transfer station is at the top of the hill on the way out of Bamfield.
  • Sites left unkept, or with toiletries left behind, are subject to a $50 cleaning fee.

Bear bins & the shared gazebo

  • Two bear-proof food bins are in the gazebo between sites 4 and 5.
  • The gazebo is shared by all campers for sheltered cooking and eating.

Pets & alcohol

  • Dogs must be leashed and never left unattended. Please pick up after your dog and keep barking down.
  • Keep alcohol in your campsite — it isn't permitted in public areas or on public beaches.

The park isn't responsible for vehicles or belongings brought into the park. Sites book up quickly in season — reserving ahead is your best bet.

Moorage at Port Desire

  • Protected moorage with a public boat launch, kayak staging, and trailer parking.
  • Space is limited. Reserve your overall boat length — bowsprit to stern gear.

Moorage & launch rates

  • Moorage: C$1.00/ft per day · C$5.60/ft per week · C$9.10/ft per month · C$66.00/ft per year.
  • Boat launch (in & out): C$20.00 · C$142.00 seasonal · C$216.00 annual.

Paying your fees

  • Pay at the office during office hours, or use the Honour Box on the hydro pole outside office hours.
  • Off-season, the Honour Box covers camping and boat-launch fees. Moorage off-season still needs to be arranged by email.
  • Trouble with the box, or no cash on you? E-transfer works too — just let us know.

More detailed moorage info (slip sizes, tides) is on the way. In the meantime, get in touch and we'll help you plan.

The park

  • Open year-round. Manager on site May 15 – Sept 30.
  • The office has the gift shop, washrooms, Wi‑Fi and picnic areas.
  • Pay at the office, or the Honour Box on the hydro pole after hours (camping & launch, off-season). No cash, or box trouble? E-transfer works too.

Parking

  • Additional angled parking is on the office side, to the right when facing the park (east), heading towards the boat launch.
  • Even more parking is available just before the boat launch.

Be bear aware

  • Store all attractants securely (in a vehicle or locker): food, coolers, garbage, pet food, BBQ grease, fish, and scented items like soap, sunscreen, and bug spray.
  • Never feed or approach wildlife, keep food in your tent, leave food unattended, or burn trash in the fire pit.

Cougars & bears live here

  • Keep your food locked up, dogs leashed, and help prevent any encounters.
Tsunami zone. The Bamfield Volunteer Fire Dept. tests the siren most Wednesdays around 6:30 PM — no need to evacuate then. If the two-tone siren sounds at any other time, head to high ground. The gathering point is the Fire Hall — don't try to drive out of town; the road runs through the tsunami zone.

Grappler Inlet Wharf · Port Desire

The wharf, from above

Tap a numbered marker to see what's what.

  1. Loading-only car park — active loading & unloading only.
  2. Boat launch — pay launch fees at the office.
  3. Kayak & canoe launch — hand-launch off the shore.
  4. Loading zone — 15 minutes max; stay with your vessel.
  5. Day-use parking — 3 hours max, for boats & day users.

Protected moorage, charged by the foot — every vessel tied or rafted to the dock is charged. Day moorage at the dock is limited to 3 hours (temporary use). No commercial vessels.

Boat-trailer parking is just up the road, at the intersection across the street.
