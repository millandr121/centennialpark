Getting here

How are you getting to Bamfield?

Three road approaches — tap a route on the map or a button below.

Port Alberni → Bamfield 90 km · about 1 hr 40 min

Chipsealed · highly recommended

★ This is the recommended route for all visitors. The Port Alberni road is fully chipsealed — a smooth, well-maintained surface all the way into Bamfield. Whether you are in a car, truck, RV, or camper van, this is the easiest and most reliable drive. First-time visitors and anyone towing a trailer or bringing an RV should use this route.

Yellow kilometre markers line the road each km so you always know how far you have to go. Mind one-lane bridges — about 60 km/h is typical. Logging trucks have right of way; drive with headlights on.

  • Fill up in Port Alberni — there is no gas station on the road.
  • Logging trucks have right of way; use headlights at all times.
  • One-lane bridge sections — slow down and yield oncoming traffic.

Duncan → Bamfield via Youbou · about 2 hr 30 min

Paved Hwy 18 + logging road

⚠ Logging road — not recommended for RVs, campers, or first-time visitors. After the Youbou area the road becomes an active logging road. Conditions vary significantly depending on recent grading — speeds can drop to 10–20 km/h on rough sections. Grading maintenance is unpredictable and not on a fixed schedule. If you are towing a trailer, bringing an RV or camper, or visiting Bamfield for the first time, we strongly advise taking the Port Alberni route instead.

Start from Duncan on Hwy 18 toward Youbou — you do not need to drive through Lake Cowichan village. Hwy 18 is paved to the Youbou area; after that, Bamfield Main is a logging road. Chipseal returns around km 32 into Bamfield.

  • Lake Cowichan — handy fuel, food, and washroom stop along Hwy 18.
  • Youbou has a gas bar; hours may be limited — fill up in Duncan when possible.

Port Renfrew / Nitinat 185 km · about 3 hr 45 min

South Island · logging road

⚠ Logging road — not recommended for RVs, campers, or first-time visitors. This route uses active logging roads from the Nitinat area into Bamfield. Road conditions are highly variable and dependent on recent grading — expect speeds of 10–20 km/h on ungraded sections. Maintenance is unpredictable. If you are in an RV, camper, or towing a trailer, or if this is your first trip to Bamfield, we strongly recommend the Port Alberni route.

Best if you are already on the south coast (Port Renfrew, Sooke, Victoria area). Paved highway to the Nitinat area, then logging road onto Bamfield Main — allow extra time; grading varies.
